The Long and Short of it.

An option is a contract giving the buyer the right but not the obligation to buy, in the case of a call, or sell, in the case of a put, the underlying asset at a specific price on or before a certain date. A stock option contract typically represents 100 shares of the underlying stock, but options may be written on any sort of underlying asset from bonds to currencies or to commodities.

Trading options, once you know how, can generate big gain. Many have become millionaires or 100x their portfolios over night on really risky options plays. On the other hand many people have also been margin called, wiped out their entire portfolio on multiple accounts, owe in some sort of fashion til this day. You must be carful and must deplay a strategy.
